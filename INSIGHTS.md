# Insights from SQLite Coding Challenge

Below are the key takeaways from the analysis of customer spending, product revenue, employee salaries, and customer loyalty.

---

## Task 1 — Top 5 Customers by Total Spend
- The top 5 customers account for a significant portion of total revenue, highlighting that a small segment of customers drives a large share of business.  
- These high-spending customers often place multiple orders with multiple items, suggesting opportunities for loyalty programs or targeted marketing campaigns.  
- There are no order status restrictions in this analysis, meaning revenue includes all orders, regardless of delivery status.

---

## Task 2 — Total Revenue by Product Category
- Certain product categories generate most of the revenue, indicating a reliance on high-performing categories for overall profitability.  
- Comparing all orders to Delivered-only orders shows slight differences in ranking, which could help identify categories prone to cancellations or unfulfilled orders.  
- Lower-revenue categories may present opportunities for price adjustments, promotions, or expanding product assortment.

---

## Task 3 — Employees Earning Above Their Department Average
- Employees earning above their department’s average may represent senior staff, specialized roles, or high performers.  
- Variation across departments indicates differing compensation structures or market-driven salary differences.  
- This insight can inform future salary planning and highlight potential equity or retention issues.

---

## Task 4 — Cities with the Most Loyal Customers
- Gold-level customers are concentrated in a few key cities, highlighting regions with strong brand loyalty.  
- Loyalty distribution by city shows geographic patterns, with some cities dominated by Silver or Bronze customers, indicating growth opportunities for loyalty programs.  
- Understanding loyalty by city allows for targeted marketing and region-specific engagement strategies.

---

## Overall Insights
- Revenue and loyalty are concentrated in small segments (both customers and locations), suggesting that targeted interventions could maximize returns.  
- Employee salary data shows potential for benchmarking and equity assessments within departments.  
- These insights can guide marketing, HR, and operational strategies to improve revenue, loyalty, and workforce planning.
